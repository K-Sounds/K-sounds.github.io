FEIM-TTS

Facial Expression-Driven Text-to-Speech Synthesis:
Innovations in Diffusion-Based Emotion Intensity Manipulation

FEIM-TTS generates speech from given text script, face image and emotion ques with intensity.

Abstract

This study proposes a zero-shot text-to-speech (TTS) synthesis model capable of generating speech that not only corresponds to facial images but also expresses emotions, with the distinctive ability to manipulate the intensity of those emotions. To our knowledge, the proposed model, FEIM-TTS. This is the first of its kind to utilize both facial images and the intensity of emotion to produce suitable voice outputs.

Moreover, our model is uniquely capable of learning and inference even in the absence of emotion labels. Consequently, we expanded our data sources to include LRS3 alongside CREMA-D and MELD, addressing the lack of audio-visual-emotional datasets. Our model also generates speech for new speakers without finetuning, ideal for virtual character voices. In terms of evaluation, we have provided evidence that the generated speech varies appropriately with the intensity of the expressed emotions. This evidence supports the effectiveness of FEIM-TTS in emotional modulation.
